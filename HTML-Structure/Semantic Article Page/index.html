Apple

Published: 06 October, 2008

An apple is an edible fruit produced by an apple tree (Malus pumila). Apple trees are cultivated worldwide and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus. The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found today.

Apples have been grown for thousands of years in Asia and Europe and were brought to North America by European colonists. Apples have religious and mythological significance in many cultures.

Apple cultivars are bred for various tastes and uses: eating fresh, cooking, and cider production. Major apple producers include China, the United States, Poland and Turkey.
